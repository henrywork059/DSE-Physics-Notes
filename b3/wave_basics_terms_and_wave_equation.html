1. What is a (progressive) wave?

  • A progressive wave is a travelling disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another.
  • For waves in a medium (e.g. rope / water / sound in air), the particles of the medium vibrate about their equilibrium positions.
  • There is no net transfer of matter over a long distance (the medium particles do not travel with the wave).
HKDSE Reminder
In explanations, use the wording “energy is transferred” and “particles vibrate about equilibrium”. Avoid saying “the wave carries the medium along”.

2. Key wave quantities (what they mean)

Core quantities and symbols

Quantity Symbol Meaning / how to identify
Amplitude \(A\) Maximum displacement from the equilibrium position.
Wavelength \(\lambda\) Smallest distance between two points that are in phase (e.g. crest to next crest).
Period \(T\) Time for one complete oscillation of a point on the wave.
Frequency \(f\) Number of oscillations per second. Unit: \(\text{Hz}\).
Wave speed \(v\) Speed of wave travel (speed of a crest / disturbance).

Extra labels commonly used in diagrams

  • Displacement: distance (with sign) from equilibrium at a given time.
  • Crest / trough (transverse): highest / lowest points of displacement.
  • Equilibrium line: where displacement is zero.
Common confusion
Amplitude is not crest-to-trough distance. Crest-to-trough distance is \(2A\).

3. Wave equation (must-know formulas)

Frequency and period

$$ f = \frac{1}{T} $$

Wave speed

$$ v = f\lambda = \frac{\lambda}{T} $$
  • Use consistent units: \(v\) in \(\text{m s}^{-1}\), \(\lambda\) in m, \(f\) in Hz, \(T\) in s.
  • In a given medium, changing \(f\) changes \(\lambda\) so that \(v\) stays the same (for the same wave type in the same medium).
HKDSE Reminder
If the source frequency doubles (same medium), the wavelength halves, so \(v\) is unchanged.

4. Reading wave graphs (exam essentials)

(A) Displacement–distance graph (a “waveform”)

  • Horizontal axis is distance at a fixed time.
  • Amplitude \(A\): maximum vertical value from equilibrium.
  • Wavelength \(\lambda\): distance between two adjacent points in phase (e.g. crest-to-crest).

(B) Displacement–time graph

  • Horizontal axis is time at a fixed position.
  • Period \(T\): time for one full cycle (e.g. crest-to-next-crest).
  • Frequency: \( f = 1/T \).

Phase (quick test)

  • Two points are in phase if they are at the same stage of oscillation (same displacement and same direction of motion).
  • On a waveform, points separated by \(\lambda\), \(2\lambda\), \(3\lambda\), ... are in phase.
Graph trap
Do not read \(T\) from a displacement–distance graph (it has no time axis). Do not read \(\lambda\) from a displacement–time graph (it has no distance axis).

5. Key terms

Wave: a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another.

Progressive wave: a travelling wave; medium particles vibrate about equilibrium while energy is transferred.

Amplitude \(A\): maximum displacement from equilibrium.

Wavelength \(\lambda\): the shortest distance between two points in phase (e.g. crest to next crest).

Period \(T\): time for one complete oscillation of a point on the wave.

Frequency \(f\): number of oscillations per second. \( f = 1/T \).

Wave speed \(v\): speed of propagation of the wave (speed of the disturbance). \( v = f\lambda \).

In phase: same displacement and same direction of motion (same stage of oscillation). On a waveform, separation of \(\lambda\) means in phase.

6. Common traps (high-frequency DSE mistakes)

Warning / Trap
  • Trap: “The wave carries the medium forward.”
    Fix: medium particles vibrate about equilibrium; energy is transferred.
  • Trap: mixing up amplitude and peak-to-peak.
    Fix: peak-to-peak (crest to trough) is \(2A\).
  • Trap: using the wrong graph to find \(T\) or \(\lambda\).
    Fix: \(T\) from displacement–time; \(\lambda\) from displacement–distance.
  • Trap: thinking higher frequency means higher wave speed (same medium).
    Fix: in the same medium, \(v\) is (approximately) constant; \(\lambda\) changes when \(f\) changes.
  • Trap: unit mistakes.
    Fix: convert cm to m, ms to s before using \( v = f\lambda \).
