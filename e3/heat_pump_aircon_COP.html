Heat pump principle

An air‑conditioner is essentially a heat pump: it transfers heat from a low‑temperature region (the indoor air) to a high‑temperature region (the outdoors) against the natural direction of heat flow【912087049771804†L5128-L5134】. It does this by evaporating a refrigerant inside the indoor coil (absorbing heat) and condensing it in the outdoor coil (releasing heat). The compressor provides the work needed to move the heat uphill in temperature.

Cooling capacity

The cooling capacity of an air‑conditioner is the rate at which it removes heat from a room, measured in kilowatts (kW)【912087049771804†L5132-L5134】. A larger capacity means the unit can cool a room faster. In the HKDSE curriculum, students are expected to interpret cooling capacity values and perform simple calculations.

For instance, a unit rated at 2.5 kW can remove 2.5 kJ of heat each second, or 2.5 kWh of heat in one hour (9 000 kJ). Oversizing wastes energy by cycling on and off frequently; undersizing may fail to maintain comfort.

Coefficient of performance (COP)

The coefficient of performance (COP) is defined as the ratio of the cooling capacity to the electrical power input【912087049771804†L5136-L5139】: \[\text{COP} = \frac{\text{cooling capacity}}{P_{\text{input}}}\, .\] A higher COP means a more efficient heat pump. Note that COP is a dimensionless number.

For example, if an air‑conditioner removes heat at 3.0 kW while consuming 1.0 kW of electrical power, its COP is 3. Typical residential units have COPs between 2 and 4. Factors affecting COP include outdoor temperature, indoor temperature and refrigerant properties. Reusing the waste heat from central air‑conditioning systems (e.g. for water heating) can further improve overall efficiency【912087049771804†L5136-L5141】.

Worked example

A split‑type air‑conditioner removes heat from a room at a rate of 2.4 kW. It draws 0.8 kW of electrical power.

Calculate:

  1. The COP of the unit.
  2. The energy removed in 3 hours.
  3. The cost of operation if electricity is HK$1.3 per kWh.

Solution:

  1. COP = 2.4/0.8 = 3.0.
  2. Heat removed: 2.4 × 3 = 7.2 kWh (26 MJ).
  3. Energy consumed: 0.8 × 3 = 2.4 kWh; cost = 2.4 × 1.3 = HK$3.12.

Reminders & Key terms

Reminders:
  • Air‑conditioners are heat pumps: they move heat from indoors to outdoors【912087049771804†L5128-L5134】.
  • Cooling capacity is measured in kW【912087049771804†L5132-L5134】; COP is a ratio【912087049771804†L5136-L5139】.
  • Higher COP means better efficiency; COP can vary with operating conditions.
Key terms
  • Heat pump – device that transfers heat against the temperature gradient.
  • Cooling capacity – rate of heat removal (kW).
  • COP – coefficient of performance, \(\text{cooling capacity}/P_{\text{input}}\).
  • Compressor – component that performs mechanical work on the refrigerant.
