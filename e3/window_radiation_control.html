Solar Control Window Film

Selective transmission of radiation for energy saving

Principle

Solar control window films consist of thin layers of metal or nano‑structured materials deposited on glass. These layers are engineered to selectively transmit visible light while reflecting or absorbing infrared and ultraviolet radiation. As a result they reduce heat gain through windows without significantly darkening the interior.

The Curriculum and Assessment Guide emphasises discussing the use of solar control window film as a measure to reduce OTTV【912087049771804†L5190-L5192】.

Note: Films can be applied to existing glazing. Some modern glass products integrate the film into the laminate during manufacture.

Benefits

By cutting down on incoming infrared radiation, solar control films lower the cooling load of buildings, which:

  • reduces energy consumption and electricity bills;
  • improves occupant comfort by minimising hot spots near windows;
  • helps protect interior furnishings from UV‑induced fading.

In commercial buildings, reducing solar heat gain contributes to a lower OTTV and helps designers meet regulatory requirements.

Selection and use

Window films come in different types, with varying levels of visible light transmission (VLT) and solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC). Choosing the right film requires balancing daylight needs with heat rejection. Factors to consider include:

  • Climate: In tropical climates, low SHGC is desirable; in temperate regions high VLT may be preferred to maximise daylight.
  • Building orientation: East and west façades receive more direct sun and benefit most from solar films.
  • Glare control: Films with lower VLT reduce glare but may require supplemental artificial lighting.

Proper installation is essential. Bubbles or improper adhesion reduce performance and appearance.

Reminders

Remember: Solar control films are not a panacea. They should be combined with other strategies such as insulation, shading devices and efficient glazing to achieve a comfortable and energy‑efficient building.
