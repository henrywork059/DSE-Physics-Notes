Advantages of public transport

Public transportation systems move many people using shared vehicles, which improves energy efficiency and reduces emissions. According to the HKDSE curriculum, students should discuss the advantages of public transportation systems and give examples【912087049771804†L5211-L5213】.

  • Higher occupancy: Buses, trains and trams carry dozens of passengers, so the energy cost per person‑kilometre is much lower than for private cars.
  • Reduced congestion: Fewer cars on the road decrease traffic jams, leading to smoother traffic flow and less idling.
  • Lower emissions: Modern public transport fleets often use electric or hybrid powertrains, further reducing pollution.
  • Space efficiency: Public transport requires less road and parking space per commuter, freeing up land in crowded cities.
Note: Encouraging public transport use requires convenient routes, frequent service and affordable fares. Urban planning and policy play an important role.

Examples

Examples of public transportation systems include:

  • Mass Transit Railway (MTR): An electrified rapid transit system widely used in Hong Kong; it offers high capacity and low emissions per passenger.
  • Electric trams and light rail: Electric streetcars are energy efficient and quiet; they can be powered by renewable electricity.
  • Buses: Double‑decker buses maximise passenger density; newer models use hybrid or battery electric drivetrains.
  • Ferries: In harbour cities, ferries provide an efficient way to move commuters and can be powered by alternative fuels.

By increasing the modal share of these systems, cities can reduce oil consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.

Reminders

Remember: Public transport is most effective when it is reliable, accessible and integrated with walking and cycling. Investing in active and shared mobility infrastructure supports sustainable development.
