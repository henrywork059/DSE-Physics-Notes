Interpreting energy labels and choosing energy‑saving devices

Overview

The Hong Kong Energy Efficiency Labelling Scheme (EELS) helps consumers choose appliances that use less electricity. Under the scheme, products such as air‑conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, dehumidifiers and televisions must display a label indicating their energy consumption and efficiency grade【912087049771804†L5146-L5153】.

A higher grade means the product consumes less electricity for the same amount of useful output. Selecting high‑efficiency devices can significantly reduce household energy bills and greenhouse gas emissions.

The energy label

The EELS label uses a five‑grade scale:

  • Grade 1: Most efficient – lowest energy consumption.
  • Grade 2: High efficiency.
  • Grade 3: Moderate efficiency.
  • Grade 4: Low efficiency.
  • Grade 5: Least efficient – highest energy consumption.

In addition to the grade, the label shows annual energy consumption (in kWh) measured under standard test conditions. Consumers can compare appliances of similar capacity by looking at both the grade and the kWh figure. A smaller number indicates lower annual energy use.

Note: Grade 1 indicates the highest efficiency; Grade 5 the lowest. The labelling scale is inverse to the numeric value. Don’t assume a higher number means a better product.

Using the label

To choose an energy‑efficient product:

  1. Identify appliances with Grade 1 or Grade 2 labels.
  2. Compare the annual energy consumption values; a lower kWh value means lower running cost.
  3. Consider the appliance’s capacity and features – very large units may use more energy even if highly efficient.
  4. Look for additional energy‑saving features, such as inverter drives, smart sensors or standby power reduction.

The curriculum encourages solving problems involving the scheme and suggesting examples of energy‑saving devices【912087049771804†L5146-L5153】. Examples include LED lighting, inverter air‑conditioners, and front‑loading washing machines.

Reminder: Some appliances are not yet covered by the mandatory labelling scheme. Always check whether a label is required for the product you’re buying.

Worked example

Two refrigerators have similar storage capacities. Fridge A has a Grade 1 label and an annual energy consumption of 320 kWh; Fridge B has a Grade 3 label and consumes 480 kWh per year. Electricity costs HK$1.3 per kWh.

Solution:

  1. Annual running cost of Fridge A: \(320\times 1.3 = \text{HK}\$416\).
  2. Annual running cost of Fridge B: \(480\times 1.3 = \text{HK}\$624\).
  3. Difference in annual cost: HK$624 – HK$416 = HK$208. Over a typical 10‑year lifespan the cost difference is about HK$2,080, illustrating the long‑term savings of a more efficient appliance.

Reminders & Key terms

Reminders:
  • Grade 1 is the highest efficiency; Grade 5 is the lowest.
  • Always compare appliances of similar capacity or output when using the label.
  • Look beyond the grade – read the annual energy consumption figure.
Key terms
  • EELS – Hong Kong Energy Efficiency Labelling Scheme.
  • Energy label – sticker showing efficiency grade and annual kWh use.
  • Energy‑saving device – appliance designed to minimise energy consumption (e.g. LED lamps).
