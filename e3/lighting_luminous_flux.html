Lighting: Luminous Flux

Quantifying the total light output of a source

Definition Measurement Worked example Reminders

Definition

Luminous flux \(\Phi\) is defined as the total energy of light emitted per unit time by a source【912087049771804†L5092-L5096】. It represents the power of visible light adjusted to the sensitivity of the human eye. The SI unit of luminous flux is the lumen (lm).

A lamp producing 800 lumens emits twice as much visible light as a 400‑lumen lamp, irrespective of their electrical power. When evaluating lamps, luminous flux tells you how much light you get, while the power rating tells you how much electricity is consumed.

Note: Luminous flux differs from luminous intensity, which is the flux emitted per unit solid angle and measured in candela (cd).

Measurement and units

The lumen is defined such that a monochromatic source emitting 1 watt of light at a wavelength of 555 nm (where the eye is most sensitive) has a luminous flux of 683 lumens. Manufacturers measure luminous flux using integrating spheres that collect all light emitted in every direction.

For a light source with luminous efficacy \(\eta\) (lm W−1) and electrical power \(P\) (W), the luminous flux is \(\Phi = \eta P\). For example, a 10 W LED bulb with an efficacy of 100 lm W−1 produces 1 000 lumens.

Worked example

An LED desk lamp consumes 6 W and has a luminous efficacy of 90 lm W−1. Find its luminous flux. How does it compare with a 40 W incandescent bulb of efficacy 12 lm W−1?

Solution:

  1. LED luminous flux: \(\Phi_{\text{LED}} = 90 \times 6 = 540\) lm.
  2. Incandescent luminous flux: \(\Phi_{\text{inc}} = 12 \times 40 = 480\) lm.
  3. Despite using much less power, the LED lamp produces more light than the incandescent bulb. It is both brighter and more energy‑efficient.

Reminders & Key terms

Reminders:
  • Luminous flux measures total visible light output adjusted for human eye sensitivity【912087049771804†L5092-L5096】.
  • Compare luminous flux to judge brightness; compare efficacy to judge efficiency.
  • Luminous intensity (candela) is flux per solid angle, not total light.
Key terms
  • Luminous flux \(\Phi\) – total light output (lm).
  • Lumen (lm) – unit of luminous flux.
  • Luminous efficacy – lumens per watt.
  • Luminous intensity – flux per unit solid angle (cd).
