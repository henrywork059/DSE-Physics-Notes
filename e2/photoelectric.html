E2 — Photoelectric Effect

HKDSE Physics — Elective E2: Atomic World · Evidence for photons and Einstein’s photoelectric equation.

The photoelectric effect supports the photon model of light. You should be able to describe the experimental observations and apply Einstein’s equation hf = φ + Kmax (and the stopping potential relation).

Exam focus: observations, threshold frequency, stopping potential Skills: use hf = φ + Kmax, Kmax = eVs Graph: Vs vs f (straight line)
1 Setup 2 Observations 3 Photon model 4 Equations 5 Graph 6 Key terms

1. Basic arrangement Setup

  • Light shines on a clean metal surface (photocathode) in a vacuum tube.
  • Photoelectrons are emitted and collected by the anode → a current flows.
  • The current is measured by a microammeter.
  • Apply a reverse p.d. to stop electrons: the stopping potential is Vs.
HKDSE note
Use of gold-leaf electroscope and more detailed apparatus is not required in the syllabus. Focus on the basic photocell arrangement and its behaviour.
Photoelectric effect: emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of sufficiently high frequency shines on it.
Photon: a packet of light energy with E = hf.
Photoelectron: electron emitted from the metal surface due to the photoelectric effect.
Work function (φ): minimum energy needed to remove an electron from the metal surface.
Threshold frequency (f0): minimum frequency of light required to cause photoelectric emission.
Stopping potential (Vs): minimum reverse p.d. needed to stop the most energetic photoelectrons.
Photoelectric current: rate of flow of photoelectrons in the circuit (depends mainly on intensity, if f is above f0).

2. Key observations Evidence

  • Threshold frequency: no emission if f < f0, no matter how intense the light is.
  • Instant emission: when f > f0, emission starts without measurable time delay.
  • Effect of intensity (at fixed f > f0): higher intensity → larger photoelectric current (more electrons per second).
  • Effect of frequency (at fixed intensity): higher frequency → larger maximum kinetic energy Kmax (and larger Vs).

3. Photon model explanation Model

  • Light consists of photons, each photon has energy E = hf.
  • One photon transfers its energy to (at most) one electron.
  • Part of the energy is used to overcome the work function φ.
  • The remainder becomes the electron’s kinetic energy (maximum value Kmax).

4. Equations you must quote Formula

hf = φ + Kmax
Kmax = eVs
hf = φ + eVs

5. Stopping potential vs frequency graph Graph

  • For a given metal, Vs increases linearly with f.
  • From hf = φ + eVs, Vs = (h/e)f - (φ/e).
  • Gradient = h/e; f-axis intercept = f0.
HKDSE note
You are not required to learn the detailed experimental method of measuring the Vs–f graph. However, using the Vs–f straight-line relationship to find quantities (e.g. work function / h) is examinable.

6. Key terms (quick list) Terms

  • Work function (φ): minimum energy to remove an electron from the surface.
  • Threshold frequency (f0): minimum frequency required for emission.
  • Stopping potential (Vs): reverse p.d. that just stops the most energetic photoelectrons.
  • Photoelectric current: depends mainly on intensity (for f > f0).
