1. What is gas pressure?

Definition

  • Pressure \( p \) is the normal force per unit area: $$ p = \frac{F}{A} $$
  • SI unit: \( \text{Pa} = \text{N m}^{-2} \).

In a gas

  • Gas pressure is due to the force on container walls from molecules colliding with the walls.
  • Each collision changes the molecule’s momentum → the wall experiences an equal and opposite impulse → force → pressure.
Exam wording
When asked to “explain pressure”, mention both: (i) momentum change per collision and (ii) collision rate.

2. Molecular explanation: what increases pressure?

At fixed volume

  • Higher temperature → molecules have higher average speed.
  • Each collision gives a larger momentum change, and collisions happen more often → pressure increases.

At fixed temperature

  • Smaller volume → molecules hit the walls more frequently (shorter distance between walls) → pressure increases.
  • More molecules (higher density) → more collisions with the wall per second → pressure increases.
Common confusion
Pressure is not caused by “weight of the gas” on the walls. It is caused by molecular impacts.

3. Kinetic model of an ideal gas (assumptions)

  • A gas contains a very large number of identical molecules moving randomly.
  • Molecules behave as point particles: their own volume is negligible compared with the container volume.
  • No intermolecular forces except during collisions.
  • Collisions between molecules and with the container walls are perfectly elastic.
  • Collision time is negligible compared with time between collisions.
When the model is reasonable
Ideal gas behaviour is closest at low pressure and high temperature (molecules far apart; forces become less important).

4. Kinetic theory relations you may use

(A) Ideal gas equation

  • For \( n \) moles at absolute temperature \( T \): $$ pV = nRT $$
  • \( R = 8.31\,\text{J mol}^{-1}\,\text{K}^{-1} \) (usually provided).

(B) Pressure from molecular motion

  • Using kinetic theory (no derivation required): $$ pV = \frac{1}{3}Nmc^2 $$
  • \( N \) = number of molecules, \( m \) = mass of one molecule, \( c \) = root mean square speed (r.m.s speed).
  • Equivalent form using density \( \rho = \frac{Nm}{V} \): $$ p = \frac{1}{3}\rho c^2 $$

(C) Temperature and average kinetic energy

  • The absolute temperature measures the average kinetic energy of molecules.
  • For an ideal gas: $$ \overline{E_k} = \frac{3RT}{2N_A} $$
  • \( N_A \) is Avogadro constant. (You can treat \( \overline{E_k} \propto T \) if only proportional reasoning is asked.)
Quick link for problem solving
If you know \( n \), use \( pV = nRT \). If you know \( N \) and \( m \), use \( pV = \tfrac{1}{3}Nmc^2 \).
Units trap
Use SI units: \( p \) in Pa, \( V \) in \( \text{m}^3 \), \( T \) in K. Convert \( \text{dm}^3 \rightarrow \text{m}^3 \) and \( ^\circ\text{C} \rightarrow \text{K} \).

5. Key terms

Pressure \( p \): normal force per unit area, \( p = F/A \). In a gas, it is due to molecular collisions with the walls.

Molecular bombardment: continuous impacts of gas molecules on the container walls, producing a net force and hence pressure.

Ideal gas: a gas that obeys the kinetic model assumptions (negligible molecular volume, no forces except during collisions, elastic collisions) so that \( pV = nRT \) holds.

Root mean square (r.m.s) speed \( c \): a measure of molecular speed defined by \( c = \sqrt{\overline{v^2}} \). It is the \( c \) used in \( pV = \tfrac{1}{3}Nmc^2 \).

Mean square speed: \( \overline{v^2} \), the average of \( v^2 \) over all molecules. r.m.s speed is \( \sqrt{\overline{v^2}} \).

Avogadro constant \( N_A \): number of particles in 1 mole of a substance. Used to link \( n \) and \( N \): \( N = nN_A \).

Real gas: an actual gas that may deviate from ideal behaviour when pressure is high or temperature is low (intermolecular forces and molecular volume become important).

6. Common traps

Warning / Trap
  • Trap: explaining pressure without momentum.
    Fix: pressure comes from rate of change of momentum due to collisions with the wall.
  • Trap: using \( ^\circ\text{C} \) in gas equations.
    Fix: always use \( T \) in K: \( T(\text{K}) = \theta(^\circ\text{C}) + 273 \).
  • Trap: wrong volume unit.
    Fix: convert to \( \text{m}^3 \): \( 1\,\text{dm}^3 = 1 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}^3 \).
  • Trap: mixing up \( n \) and \( N \).
    Fix: \( N = nN_A \). Use \( n \) with \( R \); use \( N \) with \( m \) in \( pV = \tfrac{1}{3}Nmc^2 \).
  • Trap: claiming “ideal gas has no collisions”.
    Fix: collisions do occur; they are assumed perfectly elastic.
  • Trap: saying real gases deviate because “ideal gas law is wrong”.
    Fix: deviation happens when the assumptions (negligible forces/volume) are not valid (high \( p \), low \( T \)).
