1. Meaning of temperature and a temperature scale

  • Temperature indicates the degree of hotness of a body.
  • A temperature scale assigns numbers to temperatures using fixed points and equal divisions between them.
  • Different scales use different zero points, but the size of one degree can be chosen to be the same.
HKDSE Reminder
In questions, distinguish temperature (a value on a scale) from temperature change (a difference). The conversion rule differs.

2. Celsius scale (\(^\circ\mathrm{C}\))

  • The Celsius scale is based on two fixed points at standard atmospheric pressure:
  • Ice point: melting point of pure ice = \(0\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\).
  • Steam point: boiling point of pure water = \(100\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\).
  • The interval between these points is divided into 100 equal parts (so \(1\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\) is defined by this scale division).
Exam Wording
State fixed points clearly as melting point of ice and boiling point of water at standard atmospheric pressure.

3. Kelvin scale (\(\mathrm{K}\)) and absolute zero

  • The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale with zero at absolute zero.
  • Absolute zero: \(0\,\mathrm{K}\), corresponding to about \(-273\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\).
  • On the Kelvin scale, temperatures are not negative in normal HKDSE contexts.
  • The size of one kelvin is chosen to be the same as one Celsius degree: $$ 1\,\mathrm{K} \text{ change } = 1\,^\circ\mathrm{C} \text{ change} $$
HKDSE Note
Write kelvin as K (no degree sign). For example, \(300\,\mathrm{K}\), not \(300\,^\circ\mathrm{K}\).

4. Conversion between \(^\circ\mathrm{C}\) and \(\mathrm{K}\)

Main conversion

$$ T(\mathrm{K}) = t(^\circ\mathrm{C}) + 273 $$ $$ t(^\circ\mathrm{C}) = T(\mathrm{K}) - 273 $$
  • \(0\,^\circ\mathrm{C} \approx 273\,\mathrm{K}\); \(100\,^\circ\mathrm{C} \approx 373\,\mathrm{K}\).
  • Temperature interval: $$ \Delta T(\mathrm{K}) = \Delta t(^\circ\mathrm{C}) $$ (no \(+273\) for changes).
Common Confusion
Add \(273\) only when converting a temperature value, not a temperature change.

5. Key terms

Temperature: a physical quantity indicating the degree of hotness of a body.

Celsius scale: a temperature scale with ice point \(0\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\) and steam point \(100\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\) at standard atmospheric pressure, divided into 100 equal parts.

Kelvin scale: an absolute temperature scale with zero at absolute zero; unit is \(\mathrm{K}\) (no degree sign).

Absolute temperature: temperature measured on the Kelvin scale. Conversion: \( T(\mathrm{K}) = t(^\circ\mathrm{C}) + 273 \).

Absolute zero: \(0\,\mathrm{K}\), the zero point of the Kelvin scale, corresponding to about \(-273\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\).

Temperature interval: a change/difference in temperature. It satisfies \(\Delta T(\mathrm{K}) = \Delta t(^\circ\mathrm{C})\).

6. Common traps

Warning / Trap
  • Writing \(^\circ\mathrm{K}\): Kelvin has no degree sign. Correct: \(300\,\mathrm{K}\).
  • Adding 273 to a temperature change: for intervals, \(\Delta T = \Delta t\) (do not add 273).
  • Mixing up value and unit: write \(t = 25\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\) and \(T = 298\,\mathrm{K}\) with correct symbols.
  • Sign error around 0\(^\circ\mathrm{C}\): \(-10\,^\circ\mathrm{C}\) corresponds to \(263\,\mathrm{K}\), not \(-263\,\mathrm{K}\).
  • Rounding inconsistency: use \(273\) unless the question specifies \(273.15\).
