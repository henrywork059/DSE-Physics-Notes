1. Evaporation and boiling (compare)

  • Evaporation: escape of molecules from the surface of a liquid at any temperature.
  • Boiling: rapid vaporisation occurring throughout the liquid at a fixed temperature (the boiling point) when vapour pressure equals external pressure.

Key differences you should state in exams

Evaporation Boiling
Occurs at any temperature. Occurs only at the boiling point (for a given pressure).
Occurs only at the surface. Occurs throughout the liquid (bubbles form inside).
Usually slow and no large bubbles. Rapid and bubbles of vapour form and rise.
Often causes cooling of the remaining liquid. Temperature of the liquid remains constant during boiling (if pressure is constant).
HKDSE Reminder
In explanations, emphasise surface molecules for evaporation. For boiling, mention bubble formation and constant temperature (at constant pressure).

2. Cooling effect of evaporation

  • In a liquid, molecules have a range of kinetic energies.
  • During evaporation, the more energetic molecules escape from the surface.
  • The average kinetic energy of the remaining molecules decreases → the liquid’s temperature falls.
  • Energy needed for evaporation is the latent heat of vaporisation. It is taken from the liquid (and/or surroundings).
Everyday examples
Sweating cools the body because sweat evaporates and takes energy from the skin. Alcohol/hand-sanitiser evaporates quickly and feels cold.

3. Factors affecting the rate of evaporation

  • Temperature: higher temperature → more molecules have enough energy to escape → faster evaporation.
  • Surface area: larger surface area → more surface molecules → faster evaporation.
  • Vapour density / humidity: lower vapour density above the liquid (drier air) → faster evaporation.
  • Wind / air movement: stronger wind removes vapour near the surface → lowers vapour density near the surface → faster evaporation.
HKDSE Reminder
Evaporation can occur in a vacuum. Air is not required, but the vapour density near the surface matters.

4. Latent heat of vaporisation (calculation)

  • Specific latent heat of vaporisation \( L_v \): heat needed to change 1 kg of a liquid into vapour at constant temperature.
  • Heat for evaporation / boiling: $$ Q = mL_v $$
  • For condensation, the same amount of heat is released: $$ Q = mL_v $$ (energy leaves the vapour).

Exam steps (marking-scheme style)

  • Convert mass to kg if needed.
  • Use \( Q = mL_v \) with correct units: \( Q \) in J, \( m \) in kg, \( L_v \) in J kg\(^{-1}\).
  • If a question involves a temperature change as well, combine with \( Q = mc\Delta T \) (do not mix the two formulas).
Units check
A common exam error is using grams directly in \( Q = mL_v \). Always use \( m \) in kg.

5. Key terms

Evaporation: escape of molecules from the surface of a liquid. It can occur at any temperature.

Boiling: rapid vaporisation throughout a liquid at its boiling point (at a given pressure), with bubble formation.

Cooling effect of evaporation: during evaporation, more energetic molecules escape, reducing the average kinetic energy of the remaining liquid so its temperature falls.

Specific latent heat of vaporisation \( L_v \): heat needed to change 1 kg of a liquid into vapour at constant temperature. \( Q = mL_v \).

Rate of evaporation: how fast a liquid turns into vapour (e.g. mass evaporated per unit time). It increases with temperature and surface area.

Vapour density (humidity): amount of vapour present in the air above the liquid. Lower vapour density near the surface gives faster evaporation.

Wind / air movement: wind removes vapour near the liquid surface, keeping vapour density low and increasing evaporation rate.

6. Common traps

Warning / Trap
  • Trap: “Evaporation happens only at the boiling point.”
    Fix: evaporation can occur at any temperature; boiling occurs at the boiling point.
  • Trap: “Evaporation needs air.”
    Fix: evaporation can occur in a vacuum; air mainly affects evaporation by changing vapour density near the surface.
  • Trap: “Boiling is faster evaporation at the surface.”
    Fix: boiling occurs throughout the liquid with bubbles; evaporation occurs at the surface.
  • Trap: “Wind cools because it adds cold air.”
    Fix: wind increases evaporation by removing vapour near the surface; faster evaporation causes more cooling.
  • Trap: Mixing up formulas \( Q = mc\Delta T \) and \( Q = mL_v \).
    Fix: use \( mc\Delta T \) for temperature change in one phase; use \( mL_v \) for change of state at constant temperature.
  • Trap: Sign confusion for condensation.
    Fix: evaporation/boiling absorbs heat; condensation releases heat (same magnitude \( mL_v \)).
